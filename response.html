    He/Him/His

    Amy Dallon was undoubtedly one of the most important people on Earth Bet.


    Don't think about this from the view of someone who knows about Cauldron, Contessa, Khepri, or Scion. Think about it from the perspective of an Earth Bet Native: Panacea is the girl who can heal anything except brain damage. Panacea was one of the only parahumans who actually gave back to the world, instead of continuously taking from it. Considering the amount of medical tourism she brought in, Panacea was one of the major things propping up Brockton's economy.


    Ignore the Red Queen, ignore the Wretch, ignore Earth Shin. No matter what she did, no matter who she hurt, and no matter where she went, Amy Dallon was an undeniable asset to whoever had her on their side. She could be the only cape you had, and it was still possible to pull out a win.


    She should have been guarded as jealously as a dragon hoards its treasure. She should have been guarded day and night from the shadows. A legion of bodyguards, hidden in plain sight, should have been tailing her from the minute she left her home till the minute she got back. That fact that the PRT hadn't wiped out every gang that could put her in danger in Brockton just showed Brockton's incompetence.


    He should have never gotten so close to her. He should have been found out the day he started following her, learning her routines.


    But here they were, two months after he had arrived in this accursed world, a baseball bat in his hand, and an unconscious Amy Dallon(with a rapidly growing bruise on her forehead) at his feet.


    He moved quickly, dragging her into the alleyway, hiding her from view. Tucking her behind a dumpster, he frantically looked around and grabbed her wrist.


    He needed a solid minute of full flesh-to-flesh contact for this to work.


    10 seconds.


    Where was Victoria Dallon? Where was New Wave as a whole? Why weren't they guarding Amy, the one person on their team that actually made them relevant?


    20 seconds.


    Why didn't Panacea have a driver's license or a car? Why was she skulking around in back alleys when she should be chauffeured wherever she wanted?


    30 seconds.


    Shouldn't the PRT at least be keeping tabs on her? She had undoubtedly saved the Wards and the Protectorate on dozens, maybe even hundreds of occasions. Was it too much to at least assign Shadow Stalker or Velocity to shadow her?


    40 seconds.


    What was wrong with Carol Dallon? Didn't she see the golden goose she had here? Upon triggering, Amy should have fit perfectly in the hero side of her black-and-white thinking; a girl born from a villainous father, raised by a heroic family, and given the power to heal. Victoria's powers were nothing to Amy's! Amy saved more lives in one afternoon than New Wave had done before the team was even a concept!


    50 seconds.


    Fools. All of them, fools. He wouldn't let this happen to him. He wouldn't allow himself to be taken off guard like this. If everyone, including Amy Dallon, refused to see how beautiful her power could be, how she could change the tides of this endless war of heroes and villains, then he would. He would make the world see the potential that she had.


    By taking a version of it for himself.


    60 seconds.


    He refused to hide among the filth like a dog when he had the potential to soar higher than the stars themselves.


    Power unlocked: Idle Transfiguration.


    He could feel it the moment that the power was unlocked. The way Amy's body seemed to fade away, as he realized he was looking at the girl's soul.


    And what he saw…well, he understood Kenjaku's obsession with the soul now.


    A soul wasn't just this ethereal thing hidden inside your body. Your soul was you. Your experiences in life, your memories, your feelings, your fear, your instincts, your body, the way you ran, the way you talked, the way you laughed, that feeling you got in your chest when you looked at someone you loved-


    All of that was the soul. The soul always came first. The body just came to provide it with a vessel. The body can be damaged, but the soul will stand strong. But if the soul is injured, so will the body. This was why people like Piggot, someone who was disabled, could still be considered strong and intimidating. Her body may have succumbed to its injuries, but her soul, her sense of self, stood strong.


    And Amy's soul? It was a fragile little thing. Full of doubts and insecurities, misguided lust and hate, depression and anguish. This was more effective than any analysis that Tattletale could give him, or simulation that Coil could conjure.


    This was Amy Dallon, telling him who Amy Dallon was.


    A lonely child who hated her power, but couldn't imagine a life without it. A girl who lusted after Victoria, but was intensely disgusted by it. A girl who said she wanted the power to choose her fate but would be cowed by someone she felt was suited to order her around. A coward who wanted to be brave. A healer who hated her patients.


    A walking ball of contradictions.


    Technically he had gotten everything he needed from her. All he had to do was heal her, wake her up, and pretend to be a concerned citizen, worrying about the girl he'd seen fall into the trash.


    But Amy Dallon…would still be Amy Dallon.


    The potential for the Red Queen, for the girl that would violate Victoria, was there. All it would take was a few cut strings, and boom, a monster. Jack had done it, after all. The Slaughterhouse had broken her, and quite effectively too.


    He needed to make sure that Amy wasn't a problem in the future, for him or anyone else.


    Obviously, he wouldn't fix all her issues; Amy was a competitor, after all. If he fixed her completely, and she got her head out of her ass, she would quickly outpace him, reclaiming her title as the Greatest Healer on Earth. She could affect plant matter, bacteria, and the fine minute details of the human body. All in all, it was slower than Idle Transfiguration but stronger overall.


    He couldn't have that.


    He toned down the depression. Just made it a dissatisfaction with life in general, rather than feeling the only reason she should still live is to heal. The vague fascination she got from analyzing the human body with her powers, he amped it up as high as it could go without turning it into mania. Amy would now find the human body, diseased or healthy, absolutely wonderous, marveling at the miracles of blood and bone hidden deep in people's cells. She would be like that one kid who talked about Anime all the time, even bringing it up in discussions where it wasn't needed. He doubled down on her rules, letting it feed into the fascination of the human body. The mind was utterly too fragile to go around touching; it was firmly off-limits. She wouldn't meddle in another person's head even if it meant hers-or her patients-death.


    The lust for Vicky was stamped down until it became a general attraction to blondes, and let her find females generally more attractive than men.


    Huh. Panacea was pansexual. Who would have thought? Victoria could have been a boy, girl, both, or neither, and she still would have clambered after her.


    Amy was one of the few people who could truthfully say she loved you even if you were a worm.


    Now, the obsession. The thing that made her think of Victoria twenty-four-seven, refusing to give up on her even after the world ended, and she'd been given the perfect opportunity to cut ties. He could tamp down on it, or ramp it up; it was still focused on Vicky, just without the undercurrent of lust. Or he could change its target.


    He couldn't get rid of emotions. Manipulate them? Of course. Ramp it up, squish it down, twist it, break it, play with it, all of that was possible. But he couldn't make it just go away. It was part of her soul, who she was as a person, so unless he was ready to start ripping up who Panacea was on a conceptual level, he couldn't tear it away.


    He could make her obsessed with healing, but that might lead to her breaking her rules one day, even with the new restrictions he'd given her. He could make her obsessed with justice, but that seemed like a good segue into making a good little government slave.


    Redirecting her obsession to her powers would undoubtedly backfire. He needed something that she liked that was mundane enough not to pose a threat, something she could use as stress relief, so she wouldn't go crazy. On the one hand, hobbling Panacea would ultimately make him more valuable in the cape market, but on the other, it would absolutely ruin her life.


    He was an asshole; assaulting the only halfway decent healer(who wasn't crazy) in the world for his own gain was proof of that. But he wasn't a monster.


    So he rifled through her soul, looking through things she had loved throughout her life, things that were separate from Victoria.


    There was a depressingly small amount of them.


    Princess dresses and tea parties in the snow with a large man with big brown eyes and a sharp cologne.


    A pianist, her fingers impossibly long and swift, gliding across the keyboard, making a melody that Amy could only dream of making.


    Mark on his best days, pulling her into bear hugs, ruffling her hair, cooking for the family, putting everyone in a great mood.


    Little pieces of Amy Dallon's happiness, embedded in her soul, infinitesimal little shards that made her who she was.


    Beautiful.


    But not what he needed. He pushed deeper. He needed something that she loved that wasn't a person, something similar to her awe of the pianist, a skill that she wanted to learn-


    An eight-year-old Amy, drawing for hours on end, day after day, slowly getting better with each try. She tried showing them to Carol, the woman barely sparing them a glance, but she would happily put Vicky's illegible scribbles on the fridge with pride.


    Amy gave it up soon after that.


    Artistry. Drawing and sculpting. That was an excellent way to relieve stress, and something that Amy still had a strong connection with, an experience that had embedded itself into her soul.


    He lowered the obsession to what he imagined a hobbyist would feel and switched its target from Vicky to the sound of pencils scraping against paper, the smell of paint and brushes, and the cozy feeling in her chest when a drawing in her mind came to life on the page.


    Perfect.


    He healed her and made sure that there was no lasting damage, before waking her up, helping her to her feet, and sending her on her way home


    Not that she would remember it. Until tomorrow morning, everything would be a dream for her. The changes would slowly take place over the next few days, and thanks to Idle Transfiguration, the process would be gradual enough to seem natural.


    He really couldn't believe that Mahito could do all of this, and just… didn't. Then again, the cursed spirit had only been a child when he'd been culled by Kenjaku. Given time to grow, the curse might have begun to appreciate the subtleties of his overpowered ability, but he could only wonder.


    He sent her on her way, but not before escorting her home from a distance.


    The absolute last thing he needed was some psychopath sneaking up on Panacea and bashing her brains in.


    **********************************************************


    It took about a week for him to get used to manipulating the shape of his own soul with Idle Transfiguration. He couldn't do the crazy things that Mahito could do in battle. Turn his muscle fibers into a metal-like substance? That was within his reach. Full-fledged and partial animal transformations? He did that on day two. Using his power for cosmetics, shapeshifting as both a girl and a boy, young and elderly? Easy as pie. And the best part of this was, that he didn't even have to do all of this manually. He just needed to have a clear image of what he wanted to transform into, and Idle Transfiguration would do the heavy lifting for him.


    And now that he had(somewhat) mastered it, he was onto his next target.


    Taylor Hebert.


    Make no mistake, he wanted nothing to do with the brat; Hebert was a headache and a half, and he really didn't want to be associated with her.


    He would, however, totally steal credit from her when he could. He knew all the huge events she would be involved in, so he just had to make sure he was a major part of them too. That way, whenever anyone argued about the good that Skitter or the Undersiders did, they'd have to mention his name as well.


    What good was knowing the future if you didn't leverage it as best as you could?


    No, what he wanted Taylor Hebert for was her power. Amy had given him access to Idle Transfiguration, the closest copy to Queen Shaper in the JJK Universe. If his power copying/adaptation stayed true to the same theme and universe, then there were two possible powers he could receive:


    Cursed Cockroaches, or Black Bird Manipulation.


    Cursed Cockroaches would be the closest local copy to Skitter's current powerset, but Black Bird Manipulation was a possibility purely because of Aiden, Skitter's successor. Queen Administrator mostly gave the user control over lesser lifeforms, and those two Cursed Techniques fit the bill perfectly. Either way, it wouldn't be a loss. Cursed Cockroaches would give him a utility that surpassed Taylor and would give him access to a fighting style reminiscent of Geto's/Kenjaku's, whilst Balck Bird Manipulation would allow him to keep a proper eye on the going ons of the city, as well as a one-shot attack for any non-brute in the city.


    And both powers, regardless of what he got, would give him an almost unlimited supply of minions.


    He preferred not to dirty his hands in direct combat. There were people much better suited for that kind of thing.


    Idle Transfiguration made it way easier to follow Taylor around, whether it was as a bird, a cat, a dog, or even a bug(she hadn't quite gotten to the stage where she hid insects on her body). He knew he couldn't go to her school and touch her there; even if he could shapeshift into another student, he really didn't know how to get away with touching a complete stranger for a minute without looking like a weirdo.


    What could he pose as that would let him approach anyone and not break skin contact for a whole minute without looking suspicious?


    *********************************************************


    It had actually been a pretty shitty day, overall. The Trio had done their best to get their licks in; Sophia shoving her into a wall, Madison dumping pencil shavings in her hair as she walked by, and Emma throwing a cup of milk in her face.


    Maybe eating in the bathroom wasn't such a bad idea after all.


    All in all, she was feeling pretty shitty when she sat at her bus stop and figured it couldn't get worse.


    Of course, that was when the dog appeared. A giant black mastiff, nearly as big as a bike, giving off a booming bark as it sprinted towards her, its mouth open as it showed off inch-long fangs.


    Becuase of course, her day could actually get worse.


    Fuck her life.


    But just as she covered herself as best as she could, bracing for the inevitable moment when this bear of a dog would bowl her over and start attacking her-


    It stopped.


    It just came to a stop, panting heavily as its tongue lolled out, dripping saliva as it stared at her.


    Taylor hesitantly unfolded herself, now realizing the beast of a dog wasn't going to attack her.


    It was just…waiting for something.


    An action from her?


    "Um…good dog?" she said hesitantly.


    A booming bark and a little happy jump were her answers.


    "Oh…okay. Um…sit?"


    It instantly sat on its hindquarters, giving her one hundred percent of its attention.


    "Speak?"


    BARK.


    "Roll over?"


    In a flash it was on its back, whining as it looked at her.


    Full of trepidation, she leaned forward and rubbed its stomach, ready to leap back if its behavior changed.


    She got a happy whine instead.


    "Huh. You're just a big baby, aren't you?" she said softly, a small grin appearing on her face. "You're built like a linebacker, but you just want tummy rubs, don't you? Good boy!"


    Taylor yelped as the dog moved suddenly, but the yelp of fear turned into laughter as the dog started licking her face.


    "Okay, okay, stop, that's disgusting," she giggled. "Ugh, I probably smell like dog breath now."


    The mastiff did as she asked, laying its head in her lap as it looked at her with its big, brown eyes.


    She found herself scratching its head as she waited for the bus.


    "Whose your owner buddy?" she wondered as she scratched it behind the ears. "You don't have a collar on you, but you're pretty well trained. And you're way too big to be a street dog. Is your owner nearby?"


    No replying bark this time, just plenty of drool. It was disgusting, of course, but it felt…nice, that this creature had somehow noticed her woes and come to cheer her up, like a princess in one of those fairytales.


    The heavily sanitized ones, of course.


    "You know, I was having a pretty bad day before you came along," she said softly, now scratching its chin. "I wasn't expecting something like you to come along. This is…nice."


    How long had it been since someone besides her dad had approached her with such warmth?


    She gave a huff of bitter laughter. This was pathetic; she couldn't get what she wanted from a single person in her age group, so a dog had to come and do it.


    But even if it was pathetic…it still felt really nice…


    Almost too soon, her bus pulled off, and she could already tell by the gimlet eye of the bus driver that her new friend wasn't welcome. Plus, even if she really wanted to, they didn't have the space for a dog. Not to mention the added costs that came with one.


    "You owners did a pretty good job training you," Taylor said as she got up. "So I hope they taught you what this means too."


    She pointed in the direction the dog came from.


    "Go home boy, okay?"


    An answering bellow of a bark, and her new friend trotted away.


    Taylor had never really taken care of dogs, so she could be forgiven for her lack of observation. But someone like Rachel Lindt would have noticed something…off, about the dog immediately. Like how the eyes were just a little too big and human-like. She would have noted how static the muscles beneath the skin were, almost as if they couldn't move. Rachel would have noticed that the teeth were a bit too long and a bit too sharp to belong to a dog.


    But what would have really set Rachel Lindt off was the lack of smell. Neither it's breath nor it's body gave off any scent.


    The only thing that Taylor would have noticed, when she thought about that dog months later, was how insect-free it had been…


    ****************************************************************


    "I desire iron."


    He had fucked up.


    He had honestly thought that tagging Taylor as a dog would give him access to Cursed Cokroaches, as in the ability to control enhanced cockroaches. After all, touching Amy had given him access to the Idle Transfiguration technique, not its user. But after he had fulfilled the sixty-second requirement to gain her power, the internal notification he'd gotten was quite different from what he had expected.


    Power Unlocked


    Cursed Spirit Manipulation: Kurourushi Summoning.


    At his behest, a black portal had opened in front of him, and stepping out of it was one of the ugliest things he had ever seen.


    Its face, arms, and legs looked as if they were made out of a light-colored insect exoskeleton. It was fairly tall and had four uncomfortably-looking human arms, along with hands and feet. Its abdomen was completely hidden by a black shroud that extended from its head, covering its wings and limbs as well, but they could be seen whenever the bug curse moved. Kurourushi's face was grotesque and perfectly reflected the unsightliness of an insect such as a roach. With six long antennae protruding from it, eight separate sets of eyes in different places, and human-like teeth behind its multi-layered jaw, it looked like an abomination.


    It lacked cursed energy, but it had the physical stats of a human-sized roach, the ability to slowly empower roaches that spent time around it, and it still had that weird bio-sword that implanted eggs into people.


    "I hunger, Master." the creature repeated. "I require iron for sustenance."


    He took a deep breath as he tried to see the bright side of this.


    He had a minion now, a strong one with powerful abilities that no one in the city was really prepared for. Yes, Kurourushi was disturbingly ugly, and not at all the kind of summon he would ask for first, but its power and utility couldn't be denied.


    Plus, he much preferred the idea of sending this creature into battle rather than risking himself.


    "There are rats and other vermin in the sewers of this city. You may eat as much of the vermin as you want, but gather roaches as you do so, and plant an egg of yourself in locations that are hard to access. Do not go after humans. Do not approach humans. Do not be seen. If you are seen, then flee, do not fight, even if you are attacked. No matter what, you must not alert humans to your existence unles I give you permission to do so. You will not eat any humans unless I order it. If I find that you have disobeyed me in any manner, I will exorcise you. Understood?"


    He punctuated his point by making three metal claws slide between his knuckles. Perhaps a bit of overkill, considering Kurourushi was under his complete control, but the curse was sentient; he didn't want it to try and find loopholes. Kurourushi was a special grade, and that meant he could wipe out a city in hours. He was a weak special grade, true, but a special grade nonetheless. He did not need that kind of heat on his back.


    Slowly, the curse nodded. "Understood Master. I thank you for the hunt."


    With that situation taken care of, he turned his mind to the final power that he was looking for, one that he was certain he needed before he revealed himself to the world.


    The Six Eyes.


    The thing that had made Satoru Gojo…well, Satoru Gojo.


    He firmly believed that even without an innate technique, with the Six Eyes alone, Gojo still could have matched Sukuna. Oh, the battle definitely would have been over much faster, and Gojo would have died much sooner, but the Six Eyes would have pushed the elder sorcerer just like Gojo did with his Limitless.


    Now, without cursed energy, half of the Six Eyes' utility was gone, which was the efficient use of cursed energy. But then you had the other half: eyesight comparable to a high-definition infrared camera, allowing the user to see even when their eyes were covered, allowing them to easily see things from several kilometers away and distinctly tell apart different figures within that range. But the golden ticket was that it let Gojo figure out people's cursed techniques in no time flat. Being able to figure out what your opponent's superpower was a huge asset he couldn't pass up, especially if he ever left the Bay.


    The cursed techniques that transferred over were basically the same, if only for some minor reductions. He could use Idle Transfiguration on himself as well as others, but he couldn't do it seamlessly as Mahito had, and it always took him some time to adjust to a new body, and he didn't always get the fine details right. Kurourushi's battle stats were practically the same; super strong, super fast, and super durable, but roaches in his vicinity would slowly evolve, becoming bigger, tougher, and stronger, and the curse's range seemed to be near city-wide. Six Eyes should still keep most of its ocular prowess, he'd just have to do without the Cursed Energy Efficiency.


    And the best part was, he knew where to get it, and which cape to get it from.


    ****************************************************


    Regent was a simple man. After a long hard day of doing nothing, he liked to sit back, crack open a soda, grab a cold slice of pizza, and shit-talking losers on his X-Box.


    However, when he heard the banshee wail what was Lisa's screams coming from her room, he knew his dreams of a quiet evening were fading into dust.


    "No, no, no, no, no,no!" Lisa said as she ran from her room. "I refuse to live like this!"


    "Like what, a crazy person? You do that so well though," he said with a chuckle.


    That was a mistake, he realized, as Lisa's furious eyes turned on him.


    "You," she hissed in a low tone. "This is all because of you!"


    He raised an eyebrow. "I mean, usually, it is, but can I know my crime before I'm convicted?"


    Lisa walked forward until she was almost uncomfortable close, and poked him in the chest. Hard.


    Ow.


    "Roaches."


    He blinked. "Roaches?"


    "ROACHES!" she yelled. "All over the house! In the bathroom, in my bedroom, in the kitchen! All because you can't clean up after yourself! You keep leaving food everywhere, and now we have roaches! I was trying to get a nap when I felt something crawling on me for a good minute. I get my ass up, I turn on the light, and do you wanna know what I find, Alec?"


    "...was it roaches-?"


    "It was ROACHES! Everywhere! In my blanket, under my bed, on my ceiling-!"


    Lisa gave a full-body shudder as she gagged.


    "And they're all so filthy."


    Roaches were a pretty big problem…but Alec refused to take the blame for his actions.


    "Look, are we sure there's actually roaches in the house? Maybe you just saw something weird in the dark, and your brain is confused because you took too many meds-"


    As if to contradict him on purpose, a roach the size of his hand crawled out from underneath Lisa's door.


    "Huh…Okay, we've got roaches. That's on me. We'll clean up in the morning, no biggie."


    Lisa let out a sound that sounded like a mix between a whine and a groan.


    It turned into a shriek when the thing started flying.


    *****************************************************


    He could only chuckle that night, as he watched the city below him with his new eyes, Kurourushi standing behind him as the air buzzed with the presence of millions of roaches, and a pair of wings slowly forming from his back.


    "Too fucking easy."


    Brockton was going to be his stage as he became Earth Bet's newest star.
